Common Cold/ Fever

Description:
The common cold is a viral infection of your nose and throat (upper respiratory tract). Many types of viruses can cause a common cold.
Most people recover from a common cold in a week or 10 days.
Common symptons include:
Sore throat, cough, slight body aches or a mild headache, sneezing,
low-grade fever.

Medicines:
Acetaminophen (Tylenol, Children's Tylenol),
ibuprofen (Advil, Children's Advil, Motrin), aspirin, naproxen (Aleve). 

Precautions:
Don't Expect to Take Antibiotics.
Do Get Plenty of Rest. Don't Forget to Drink Water.
Do Keep Your Cool. 

Stomache Ache

Description:
Pain from inside the abdomen or the outer muscle wall, ranging from mild and temporary to severe and requiring emergency care.
Common Causes:
Abdominal pain can have causes including constipation,
wind, overeating, stress or muscle strain.

Self-treatment:
Taking an antacid and eating smaller meals may help to get relieve.
See a doctor immediately if you:
Feel severe pain that worsens with movement develop a fever,
vomit or have bloody diarrhoea or experience abdominal swelling.

Precautions:
It's best to avoid taking aspirin or ibuprofen as these medications can worsen some abdominal pain.
Drink plenty of Water.

Minor Cuts & Wounds

Description:
Minor cuts and scrapes usually stop bleeding on their own. If needed, apply gentle pressure with a clean bandage or cloth and elevate the wound until bleeding stops. Clean the wound. Rinse the wound with water.
Apply a thin layer of an antibiotic ointment or petroleum jelly to keep the surface moist and help prevent scarring.
Apply a bandage, rolled gauze or gauze held in place with paper tape.
Remove any dirt or debris with a tweezers cleaned with alcohol.
See a doctor if you can't remove all debris.

Precautions:
Wash your hands. This helps avoid infection.
Don't use soap rich in hydrogen peroxide or iodine, which can be irritating.
